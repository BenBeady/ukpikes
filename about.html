Who We Are

Pi Kappa Alpha — Omega Chapter

The Omega Chapter of Pi Kappa Alpha at the University of Kentucky has a long tradition of developing leaders, scholars, and community contributors on campus and beyond.

Founded in 1901, our brotherhood uphold the four core values of Scholarship, Leadership, Athletics, and Gentlemanly Conduct. We are active on campus through BBNvolved and engage in recruitment, philanthropy, and professional development year-round.

Pi Kappa Alpha was founded nationally on March 1, 1868 at the University of Virginia. Today, we are one of the largest fraternities in the world with over 225 chapters and 310,000 lifetime initiates.

Our Foundation

Four Core Values

01

Scholarship

Academic excellence is a cornerstone of Pike. We maintain one of the highest chapter GPAs in the IFC with study support and scholarship recognition.

02

Leadership

From SGA to campus organizations, Pike brothers lead across the University of Kentucky. We develop the next generation of leaders in every field.

03

Athletics

Intramural champions and varsity participants alike, Pike promotes physical fitness and competitive spirit both on and off the field.

04

Gentlemanly Conduct

Integrity, respect, and high moral character define a True Pike. We hold our brothers to the highest standard of conduct in all areas of life.
